Redesigning Mel.org for Librarians

The Challenge: Redesign the state e-library portal for librarians as in Michigan state as the primary target audience.
The Outcome: Over the semester my team and I came up with detailed recommendations and six thorough user experience research reports for the Mel.org team. These recommendations were appreciated by the team and are already live on the website. Note: The screenshots here are from the previous version of the website, i.e from Feb 2018.
Spoiler: By the end of this three month long research project my team and I had created six 2000 word reports, 5 slideshows, and a video summarizing our findings.
My Role: UX Researcher, UX Writer
Team Members: Mandy Shen and Shaung Cheng
Project Timeline: January 2018 - April 2018

Overview

In February of 2018, I got together with two other UX students from the University of Michigan to work with the Michigan eLibrary to explore the user experience of their website especially from the perspective of public librarians in the state of Michigan.

To understand and scope the problem we framed a few high level research questions

  • How might we make the resources on MeL more accessible for librarians?
  • How might we improve the flow of information from Librarians to the society?
  • How might we minimize variations in experience of using MeL among users with less or no exposure to digital technology?

The following sections talks in detail about our process and challenges.

Methodology

Over the semester we applied User research methods as shown in the flowchart to explore the framed research questions and generate insights at each level. These recommendations worked as input for the next and were also helpful in gathering feedback from the client on a biweekly basis. Thus in the end we had refined recommendations which are now live on the website.

Interaction Map for Mel.org

Before redesigning interactions for Librarians we decided to understand major workflows on the websitea and ways in which Librarian interacts with them. The goal was to identify break points and get an overall understanding of the website's information architecture.


Zooming in on the Librarian sections


From the interaction map we learned that a wide variety of online training material is available specifically for librarians. They can use these tools and training to promote and get the best use out of Michigan eLibrary databases, portal resources, and Michigana (http://MeL.org/promotions).

Librarians in Michigan are the face of MeL for major section of the society like the elderly and school students. They are aware of what the local community needs and hence looking closely at their experience with MeL can guide us towards insightful findings to improve overall usability of Mel.org too.

    To assess the functionalities and quality of MeL specific to librarians (from a user’s perspective instead of features) we selected six competitors.

  • Survey

    To investigate how effective the current sections on MeL are in supporting librarians we sent out a survey to librarians working at public libraries in Michigan State.

  • Heuristic Evaluation

    With many heuristics available we decided to pick one of the most popular ones, Nielsen's heuristic to evaluate and find usability issues in MeL.


Interviews

After the four interviews, we reviewed the notes taken, listened to the audio recordings and turned the audio into transcribed notes. To reinforce the process, we also used IBM Speech to Text API to help us finalize the transcript. We adopted Open Coding method to analyze our interview data in excel.

First we looked at each interview, then each theme, and eventually discovered overall insights.

Competetive Analysis

Through the competetive analysis we learned that by looking at other services in the elibrary space MeL can improve its search functionality, update to a consistent as well as intuitive visual design and use content specific categorization in the navigation bars.

Survey

According to the clien (who distributed our survey through library channels) our survey reached all public libraries in Michigan and we are confident that this sample is able to represent our target audience.

The results of a few multiple choice questions were not as reliable because participants who chose “Other” didn’t all elaborate on their choice although corresponding text entry boxes were provided. Though we did a textual analysis of the “Other” answers it did not give us clear objective data we had expected.

Heuristic Evaluation

Our heuristic evaluation involved several steps: first we defined the task and use cases on MeL. Next, we defined heuristics, variables and severity. Then, we evaluated heuristics individually, and combined all the results at the end.

Results

1. MeL has a vast pool of resources. However, most librarians find it hard to navigate or find desirable information.
Provide supportive information that is prominent to users on different pages. The information could be short videos or articles to education users the best way to use MeL. Design a more in-depth navigation with clear information hierarchy to help users quickly locate the information they need.
2. Promotion and Training section is hard to navigate because resources are not organized the way users expect. Some sub sections have over than 20 entries, while some have only one entry.
Clean up duplicated resources and categorize them by topic. Some possible headers could be: Tutorials, Workshop, and Promotion material. “Librarians love filters” : Provide filters to choose different types of resources like video/article/website. Consider add a search bar for Promotion & Training section.
3. The search function on Mel confuses users.
Visually emphasize the message of different search bars, build a search function that searches the whole site, and remove the conflicting message on the promotion and training pages.
